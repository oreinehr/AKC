4.2.2

Phosphor — the lume rule

A watch dial has one signature color and a separate lume — a luminous compound that only activates in defined conditions. Phosphor works the same way. Fill only, foundation black on top, roughly one surface in ten.

lume · fill only #D8FF3D

pantone 389 c

rgb 216 · 255 · 61 — hsl 70° · 100% · 62% — cmyk 15 · 0 · 76 · 0

the rule

Present, structural, but only visible in defined contexts — and always with foundation black sitting on top.

appears in
Time-stamps · kinetic showcases · single hero highlights · ai-content tags · generative samples · hover states on dark surfaces.
never in
Stationery · email signature · print proposals · as a text color · the logo.
budget
~1 surface in 10. If it lives everywhere, it lives nowhere.
4.2.3

Gradients

Four gradients built strictly from the primary system plus phosphor. Two are locked into hero / dark-mode use; two are situational and count against the lume budget when phosphor is involved.

Kinetic

tier · locked

--gradient-kinetic — linear-gradient(90deg, #0047FF → #D8FF3D)

Hero kinetic moments, motion intros, generative title cards. Loud-register signature.

Depth

tier · locked

--gradient-depth — linear-gradient(135deg, #0F1115 → #0047FF)

Dark-mode hero, video lower-third backgrounds, premium content tiles.

Tonal

tier · situational

--gradient-tonal — linear-gradient(135deg, #1B2A4E → #0047FF)

Backgrounds, photography overlays, deep-color washes. No phosphor — free to use.

Drama

tier · situational · counts vs. lume

--gradient-drama — linear-gradient(90deg, #0F1115 → #D8FF3D)

Rare drama only. Counts against the phosphor budget — earn it before you spend it.

4.2.6

Hard rules

If the palette breaks, it breaks here. Six rules, no exceptions, no soft enforcement.

do

  • 01. Use the four primary tokens for ~90% of every surface. The system is small on purpose.
  • 02. Reach for accent-loud for digital UI, accent-quiet for print and body emphasis. They are a register pair, not interchangeable.
  • 03. Place phosphor only as a fill — and always with foundation black sitting on top.
  • 04. Treat the four gradients as a finished library. Build with them, not around them.
  • 05. Specify Pantone for any printed surface; CMYK only when Pantone is unavailable.

don't

  • 01. Use phosphor as a text color. Ever. On any background.
  • 02. Add a new color. The palette is closed — extend by rhythm, scale or composition instead.
  • 03. Recolor the logo to phosphor or to accent-loud. Black or white only.
  • 04. Pair accent-loud as body copy on foundation — fails AA at small sizes. Large display only.
  • 05. Let phosphor cover more than ~1 surface in 10 across a campaign. Scarcity is the source of its weight.
