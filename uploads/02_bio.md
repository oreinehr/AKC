# Bio Trio

> Three bios from the same source: locked positioning + brand purpose (freelance pipeline + senior-career portfolio).
> Posture rule across all three: **confident and currently-doing-the-work. Never looking-for-work.**
> Variant choice tracks context: *Built for scale* in proposal/press registers; *Built for the long game* in relationship registers.
> AI = background mention, not headline. Tools change; standards don't.

---

## Decisions baked in

- **Opener for medium and long bios:** proof phrase as cold open. Highest-leverage first sentence we have.
- **Order of proof:** philosophy → calibration sources → outcomes → current role. Standards-led, not job-title-led.
- **Length philosophy:** tight is more senior than verbose. Restraint is on-brand.
- **Language:** EN-first. Portuguese versions to follow once EN is locked (idiomatic adaptation, not literal).

## 01 — Short bio (LinkedIn headline)

LinkedIn headline space — character budget ~220, but real readability budget ~120. Role keywords kept for searchability (recruiters at SAP-tier search "Creative Director" and "UX Designer"). Variant: *Built for the long game* (LinkedIn = relationship channel).

**Primary:**
> Creative Director · Brand & UX · Calibrated by craft. Built for the long game.

**Alternates:**

A. *Creative Director & UX Designer · Calibrated by craft, built for the long game · BR ↔ US*

B. *Creative Director · UX & Brand · 20 years across Brazil and the U.S. · Calibrated by craft.*

C. *Creative Director · Brand & UX Systems · Calibrated by craft. Built for the long game.*

**Notes.**
- Current role ("Creative Director at TGR Studio") shows automatically beneath the headline from the Experience section — don't waste headline real estate repeating it.
- Keep the lead phrase intact; LinkedIn truncates after ~70–80 characters in some surfaces, so the most important words go first.

## 02 — Medium bio (LinkedIn About / one-pager hero)

For LinkedIn About and the one-pager landing page hero/about block. Length: ~210 words — tight on purpose. Variant: *Built for the long game.*

> *Technology evolves. Excellence doesn't.*
>
> I'm Alessandro Kuhn Contes — a Creative Director and UX Designer working between Brazil and the United States. Currently leading creative at TGR Studio.
>
> The work sits at the intersection of brand, UX, and creative leadership. What makes it hold up across formats and decades isn't taste or trend — it's a calibration bar tuned in cultures that don't tolerate slack: Swiss horology, techno purism, tennis, twenty years of constant education. Scaling that calibration into enterprise work is the practice.
>
> Some of what I've shipped:
>
> - 274% productivity lift inside K2 (K1 Group), in twelve months.
> - 25% revenue growth at TGR through rebuilt client trust and long-term partnerships.
> - Public-facing campaign work for NYC Service, distributed across the New York City subway system.
>
> AI is a teammate, not a tagline. It's been part of the practice for years — so the question I care about isn't *whether* it's in the work, but *whether the work holds to a higher bar than the tools alone would produce.*
>
> *Calibrated by craft. Built for the long game.*
>
> hello@akcontes.com

**Why this version works for both audiences (freelance buyer + senior recruiter):**

- Names current senior role (TGR) → recruiter-readable seniority signal.
- Leads with philosophy → freelance buyer reads taste and conviction first.
- Numbers in the middle → both audiences want quantified outcomes.
- Closes on brand language + email → no "let's talk" CTA, no "open to work" flag. Statement of brand. Take it or leave it.

**One-pager hero variant.** When used as the hero block on a landing page, drop the closing email line (the page already has contact) and consider tightening to the proof phrase, the first paragraph, and the bullets — about 100–120 words above the fold.

## Reusable template (for future client work)

This bio file is the template. The structure that transfers:

1. **Decisions baked in** (so the next person — or future you — can see why each choice was made).
2. **Three lengths.** Headline, About, Press. Anything shorter than headline is a tagline; anything longer than press is a profile piece, not a bio.
3. **Variant tracking.** Each bio names which lead-phrase variant it uses and why — so the brand stays coherent across surfaces.
4. **Posture rule** stated at the top. Without it, bios drift toward résumé tone.
5. **Closing brand language** instead of CTAs. CTAs in bios read junior. Brand statement reads senior.
