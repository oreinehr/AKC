## 3. Strategic problem to solve

Most senior creative directors brand themselves as either "design-led strategist" or "strategy-led designer" — interchangeable. The brand needs to signal seniority and a real point of view without slipping into agency boilerplate.

It must travel across:

- **Two markets:** Brazil and the United States.
- **Two audiences:** enterprise clients and design talent.
- **Two registers:** formal stationery and conversational LinkedIn.

Personal-texture signals (Section 2) are raw material for *differentiation*. Use them where they sharpen, set them aside where they distract.

## 4. Meta-goal

This rebrand is also a training ground. I'm learning `.md` files, Claude Cowork, Claude Code, and Claude Design *through* this project so I can sell the same engagement to clients later.

Every step is a reusable template. Name the patterns. Flag what transfers. Structure outputs as files that hand cleanly to the next client.

## 5. Fixed constraints

## 6. Deliverables (priority order)

1. **Positioning phrase** — 3–5 distinct directions, each with its underlying thesis. Explicit about which personal-texture threads each leans on.
2. **Bio** — short (LinkedIn headline), medium (LinkedIn About / one-pager hero), long (speaker / press).
3. **Color palette** — print (CMYK / Pantone) and digital (HEX / RGB / HSL) systems with usage rules and accessibility notes.
4. **Typography** — display + text pairings with rationale, fallback stacks, licensing for print and web.
5. **Stationery & email signature** — letterhead, business card, HTML email signature.
6. **One-pager landing page** — single-file HTML, host-anywhere.
7. **LinkedIn rework** — banner, headline, About, Featured, Experience rewrites, post-cadence starter kit.
8. **Byproducts** — 2–3 recommendations beyond the list above.

## 7. Quality bar

Originality over polish. One risky, opinionated direction beats three safe ones. When options are presented, they should be different **bets**, not different **flavors**.

- Every output saved as a file in the project folder.
